What is the eventual plan with the real-world recreations?

The end goal is to create large-scale, visitable, physical spaces based on fictional (or liminal) worlds. They won't just be theme park rides or decorated rooms, but fully explorable environments. Imagine wandering through the infinite yellow halls of the Backrooms, or exploring every corridor of the Hogwarts castle.

What is the timeline of the project looking like?

We can't really say at this point; we're still in the first stage. The project is designed as a long-term, multi-stage process. When we start building our second or third real-world creation could be weeks or months or even years after the first or second.

Why start with the Backrooms and not Hogwarts?

The Backrooms is an ideal one to start with: the environments are simpler to design and build, they're highly flexible, and they're in the public domain (so no licensing barriers!). Hogwarts is much more complex, requires "fact"/correctness, and requires licensing, so beginning with the Backrooms lets us start smaller while still creating something exciting.

So... just to be clear, is this just digital, or will it really become physical?

It's both. The digital projects are the starting point; proofs of concept that let people see the vision. But the ultimate goal has always been physical builds: real, explorable spaces where people can step directly into these worlds.

How will this be funded?

Funding will come through a mix of approaches: Crowdfunding, community support, potential investors, and partnerships. Starting with digital projects helps build a following and demonstrate proof of interest, which makes it easier to secure larger-scale funding later.

Isn't licensing an issue (especially with ones like Hogwarts)?

Yes. Projects tied to specific intellectual property (like Hogwarts) will require licensing or official partnerships. That's why our early work focuses on original or public-domain concepts such as the Backrooms. These allow us to make progress while we build the traction needed for more complex, licensed worlds in the future.

Who's working on this?

Right now, F2R is run by a small but growing creative team. Our skills include 3D modeling, 2D animation, storytelling, worldbuilding, design, entrepreneurship, and more. As the project grows, so will the team. We plan to bring in more creators, builders, and supporters over time.

How can I get involved or support the project?

The easiest way to support right now is to follow our content, share it, and spread the word. As we grow, we'll launch crowdfunding campaigns and open up ways for fans and creators to directly participate in shaping the project.
